I went on a HOLIDAY and here's what I did

Is it classified as a holiday if I just went home? What does Google say ... OK, Google says "an extended period of leisure and recreation, especially one spent away from home or in travelling." This is vague. I mean, I went back HOME on a holiday, well in the near vicinity of my home. I went to Taronga Zoo and saw some animals. I feel bad a little. I paid for the animals to be caged and I looked at them while they were trapped. The zoo-keepers spoke a lot about conservation and all that stuff, but ... they're still trapped. I actually don't know much about this topic at all, so I'm not judging or anything, I'm just saying I felt a little bit bad. I wonder if they rotate the giraffes. Like, it would be so boring to be a giraffe in the zoo that did the same thing all the time. Wake up, get fed, get looked at and then sleep.
The giraffes I saw were so cute. I brought my camera to the zoo and took photos of some of the animals. The most remarkable animal was the giraffe! Their necks were so freaky long and their enclosure was right above the Sydney Harbour Bridge and Opera House. It's a little but funny, considering how tall they are and they were given the best view. I feel like a smaller-necked animal would be better suited for that place. You could place the giraffes anywhere and they could still see the view. ARE YOU PICKING UP WHAT I'M PUTTING DOWN?!
So, other than the giraffes, what else did I see or do? I also saw a lovely pygmy hippo.
It would be so refreshing to be a pygmy hippo ... because first of all, you're always swim swim swimming around and second of all, you actually are like super hairless, so you would glide in the water so easily. The worst thing about washing your hair is that you have damp hair. I actually have damp hair right now. I'm sitting here, with my wet hair propped up on my head in a bun, held up by a shitty Cotton-On hair clip that is kinda broken. The clip's teeth is like wonky because I put it in my gym bag and I think I kicked my gym bag or something and the teeth went wonky. It still works, but I probably wouldn't ever sell it on Depop. Back to the animals. I also saw a tiger. I read a thing - actually, I watched a TikTok, but this sounds better - and it was talking about how tigers camoflauge. The tiger is orange, but its environment is not orange. So, how does it blend in? Well, turns out our human eyes perceive colours differently than other animals! This is actually so obvious, but we totally forget things like this. It's like when Americans forget that there are non-Americans in the world and their whole life view shatters. I took a photo of the tiger when it was sleeping on the car. I'll put all my Taronga Zoo pics in the bottom right corner grid.
